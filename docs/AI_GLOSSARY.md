# AI Glossary

- **Par Level** — target stock level a kitchen wants on hand.
- **Yield** — usable output after trim, cooking, or processing.
- **Prep Item** — an in-house prepared component used later in dishes.
- **Raw Stock** — purchased ingredient inventory not yet converted into prep output.
- **Finished Goods** — ready-to-sell or ready-to-serve stock. Needs Verification: exact repo-wide distinction vs `stockType` usage.
- **Food Cost %** — the share of selling price consumed by ingredient/prep cost.
- **COGS** — cost of goods sold; ingredient cost consumed by sales or production.
- **Service Readiness** — a weighted operational score indicating whether the kitchen is ready to serve.
- **Dishes At Risk** — menu items whose required prep or ingredients are insufficient for expected covers.
- **Supplier Cutoff** — the latest time an order can be placed for a supplier.
- **Waste Event** — a logged spoilage, overproduction, or discard event with cost impact.
- **Stocktake** — physical count used to reconcile expected vs actual stock.
- **HACCP** — food safety process controlling hazards via tracked checks like temperature logs.
- **FIFO** — first in, first out; older stock is consumed before newer stock.
- **MSDS / SDS** — supplier safety documentation for chemicals.
- **Morning Workflow** — command-centre action that turns covers into prep/readiness signals and optionally creates prep tasks.
- **Menu Mix** — the assumed split of covers across active menu items.
- **Stagnant Stock** — stock that has not moved for long enough to become an operational concern.
